It’s the Economy, Stupid

A Call to the Democratic Party to re-embrace Millennials and Gen Z.

22 February 2026

Introduction

The 2024 election revealed a stark reality: many Americans prioritize immediate economic concerns and surface-level perceptions over more abstract, theoretical debates about governance, ethics, and leadership. While moral and cultural issues have dominated political discourse, voters focused primarily on inflation, economic "vibes," and the perceived vitality of the economy. To regain the trust and support of moderates, Millennials, and Gen Z, the Democratic Party must shift its focus toward a bold, visionary economic agenda that inspires and empowers.

The Lessons from 1992

During Bill Clinton's presidential campaign in 1992, James Carville succinctly coined the phrase "It's the economy, stupid" to emphasize that it is the importance of economic issues that win elections. Clinton's focus on economic recovery and job creation resonated with voters, leading to a decisive victory. This lesson remains relevant today: voters are far more likely to support candidates who address their economic concerns directly and offer tangible solutions for improving their financial well-being.

What Voters Chose and What They Ignored in 2024

We saw this very clearly in 2024 when a majority of Americans voted for Donald Trump driven by concerns about inflation and economic stability. Many overlooked or outright dismissed more distant issues such as corruption, incompetence, mismanagement, the mishandling of the COVID-19 pandemic, and the weaponization of government institutions. This selective focus on economic "vibes" over governance challenges determined the election outcome.

The Economic Vision America Needs

Project 2029 and similar initiatives have emphasized moral stands that, while important, clear do not resonate as the primary concerns for a majority of voters. This disconnect continues to contribute to electoral setbacks!

To win back the future, Democrats must champion a comprehensive economic vision that prioritizes:

  • Investment in Education: Affordable, accessible education is the foundation for innovation and opportunity. Millennials, Gen Z, and even Gen Alpha must be encouraged to invest in their futures through lifelong learning and skill development. Education opens doors to the industries of the future, enabling young people to earn living wages, buy homes, and raise families.
  • Advancement in Science and Technology: America’s global leadership depends on breakthroughs in technology, clean energy, healthcare, and infrastructure. Bold funding and policy support can drive innovation that creates jobs and strengthens the economy.
  • Dreaming Big to Build: Infrastructure projects, green energy initiatives, and technological advancements must be pursued with ambition and scale, signaling a commitment to growth and prosperity.

A Call to Action

This generation stands at a crossroads. By embracing education, science, and technology, Millennials, Gen Z, and Gen Alpha can reclaim their futures and restore American greatness. Investing in education leads to careers in emerging industries, providing the economic stability necessary to achieve personal milestones like homeownership and family-building. This is a call to action to dream boldly, invest wisely, and build a nation that reflects their aspirations and potential.

Conclusion

The path forward requires a shift from abstract moral debates to concrete economic empowerment. By selling a compelling economic vision rooted in education, innovation, and large-scale ambition, the Democratic Party can reconnect with the voters who matter most and lead America toward a brighter future.
