About Me

The Perspective of a 21st Century Builder

I am a web developer based in Charlotte, NC, but I have always been a student of the long view.

In 2017, I ran for Charlotte City Council in a heavily contested suburban district. I didn't run because I wanted a career in politics; I ran because I saw the cracks in the foundation. I saw a political class more interested in fighting 19th-century culture wars than in preparing our citizens for the 21st century global economy. I ran because I wanted to be part of the solution, and I wanted to help build a better future for my community.

From the Campaign Trail to the Codebase

During the campaign, I advocated for a pragmatic, Blue Dog approach to governance, emphasizing fiscal responsibility and infrastructure investment. I believed that by focusing on these core issues, we could create a more prosperous and equitable city for all residents.

Today, my perspective is shaped by the logic of software development. I see the world as a complex system of interconnected parts, and I believe that by understanding how these parts work together, we can build better solutions to the problems we face. In code, you can't ignore reality; if your logic is flawed, the system fails. I believe the same is true for our nation.

I believe in a government small enough to stay out of your private life, but strong enough to build the world-class infrastructure and education systems we need to compete with the likes of China and the European Union.

Why I Write

I believe America is currently caught in a crisis of institutional trust and a dangerous slide into nostalgia. While some hope to return to a world of manual labor and isolationism, I see a horizon defined by:

  • Knowledge-Based Leadership: Ensuring every North Carolinian has the education to lead in an automated world.
  • Global Connectivity: Reclaiming America's role as the world's provider of healthcare, technology, and democratic ideals.
  • Privacy: Protecting the individual's right to live, love, and choose without government interference.

The Blue Horizon Vision

I am a Millennial, a member of the generation tasked with rebuilding our civic order. I believe we don't need more "doublespeak" or "smokestack nostalgia". We need a New Progressive Era that embraces the realities of the 21st century while upholding the core values of freedom, opportunity, and community. I call this vision the Blue Horizon, a future where we build a society that is both innovative and inclusive, where technology serves humanity, and where every individual has the opportunity to thrive.
